# Setting

The state of the Alpha Quadrant in 2378, the Maelstrom region, the
Vesuvi incident that opens the campaign, Starbase 12, and the
classified background to the *Sovereign* refit project.

---

## Alpha Quadrant — recent history (as of mid-2378)

The Federation is roughly six months past the **surrender of the
Dominion**. After the war, Starfleet, the Klingon Empire, and the
Romulan Star Empire formed a peacekeeping coalition.

Wartime collaborators (races and factions that had sided with the
Dominion to seize allied territory) were sanctioned heavily:

- The Klingon and Romulan Empires each imposed a strict **no-buildup
  policy** on every former Dominion-allied power.
- Special restrictions were placed on the **Cardassian Union**.
- Cardassian, Breen, Gorn, and other defeated Alpha-Quadrant powers
  faced calls for hard penalties; the Federation has resisted those in
  favour of reconstruction.

The Federation continues to negotiate with the Klingon, Romulan, and
Cardassian governments. **Diplomacy has not been very successful**:
accusations persist of forces hiding in violation of treaties and of
secret military caches. Tensions between the major Alpha-Quadrant
powers are the dominant feature of the period.

**This is the geopolitical pressure the Dauntless mission unfolds
under.** The Dominion may be defeated, but a careless incident could
restart hostilities at any time.

## The Maelstrom

A volatile region of space on the **edge of Federation, Klingon and
Cardassian territories**. Catalogued by Starfleet but not contested by
any major power because of how inhospitable it is.

### Defining hazards

- **Triple-ambient stellar radiation** — most stars within the
  Maelstrom produce roughly 3× the radiation of normal M-type stellar
  environments.
- **Subspace tears.**
- **Quantum destabilisation.**
- **High background radioactivity.**

### Federation activity within

- The science vessel **USS Hawking** ran the initial survey.
- Several leading Federation scientists pushed for permanent research
  posts inside the region.
- Recent advances (atmospheric shielding, environmental bio-filters)
  finally made limited terraforming possible — within the last two
  years, two outermost planets in the Vesuvi system were terraformed
  to enable mineral mining.
- The **Draegos colony** on Vesuvi 3 was being built up as the first
  Federation science post inside the Maelstrom proper at the time of
  the Vesuvi incident.

### Travelling rules

Even with current Starfleet technology, the Maelstrom is **classified
hazardous**; vessels are advised to take extreme care when traversing
any system inside it.

### Notable systems & operating units inside the Maelstrom

| System / station | Status |
|---|---|
| **Vesuvi system** | Site of the inciting incident; Draegos colony on Vesuvi 3, Haven on Vesuvi 6, Geki Station on Vesuvi 5. |
| **Tau Ceti Prime** | Three Starfleet drydocks orbit it, alongside Soho Station; serves as the primary repair yard for ships in the region (incl. *Nightingale* and *Dauntless* refits). |
| **Biranu system** | Biranu Station (Regula-class) orbits Osa / Biranu 2. |
| **Savoy system** | Savoy Station (Regula-class) orbits Savoy 1. |
| **Tevron, Beol, Belaruz, Artrus, Goralis, etc.** | Patrol / combat-ops sectors; various ships sighted. |
| **Starbase 12** | Federation operations centre adjacent to the Maelstrom; orbits New Holland. |

## The Vesuvi incident

The single event that sets the campaign in motion.

### Lead-up (manual's "Introduction" fiction, pp. 2–5)

- The *Dauntless* has just completed a baryon sweep at the Remmler
  array at **Arkaria base** for badly needed maintenance after the
  Dominion War.
- Captain Wright receives orders from Admiral Liu: routine resupply run
  to the **Vesuvi system**, plus an investigation into the system's
  "odd stellar behaviour". Federation scientists on Vesuvi 3 had
  filed inconclusive readings and wanted a Starfleet ship to take a
  look.
- The *Dauntless* arrives in the Vesuvi system. Wright takes the
  shuttlecraft *Icarus* down to the Draegos colony (with a personal
  surprise from his daughter — Romulan Ale — packed in his duty
  case for his son-in-law Chief Serson).
- He hands the conn to the player ("**Commander, you have the
  Conn**"). After six months as XO the player has earned trust.
- The colony is barely six months into atmosphere processing.
  Engineers Chief Serson and Lt. Nathan James are routing ODN conduits
  for an atmosphere generator. Communications officer Jordan calls
  them in for their Hyronalin radiation hypos.
- Then **something happens to the Vesuvi star**.

### Aftermath

- Captain Wright is killed (cf. Admiral Liu's letter of condolence
  dated **2378-06-17**, addressed to "Acting Captain and Crew, USS
  Dauntless").
- The **player is now acting captain** of the *Dauntless*.
- Geki Station (Vesuvi 5) is severely damaged and abandoned.
- A memorial service is held at Starbase 12 seventy-two hours after
  the incident — chapel service, ship review, wreath-casting.
- Lt. Susan Wright-Serson (Wright's daughter, on Starbase 12) requests
  in lieu of synth-flowers that condolence credits go to the **Starfleet
  Academy General Scholarship Fund** in her father's memory.

The campaign begins from this state: a Galaxy-class starship with a
seasoned crew that has lost its captain, operating in the most volatile
sector of explored space, in a postwar political climate where any
misstep can pull the major Alpha-Quadrant powers back into conflict.

> See [`characters/captain-wright.md`](characters/captain-wright.md)
> for Wright's record, and [`characters/supporting-cast.md`](characters/supporting-cast.md)
> for Liu, Wright-Serson, Serson, and the Vesuvi colonists.

## Starbase 12

The *Dauntless*'s operations centre, located along the
Federation/Cardassian border.

- **Orbits**: New Holland (Tau Ceti Prime sector, near the Maelstrom).
- **Class**: Federation Starbase (largest class — 4,600 m diameter,
  6,950 m height, 1,200 decks, displacement ~236 million tons).
- **Complement**: 15,000 Starfleet personnel + 20–35,000 civilians +
  6,625 transients.
- **Defences**: 8× RIM-12C Independent Twin Mount Phaser
  Emplacements; high-capacity shield system rated at ~75.6 million
  terajoules; hull rating 220.
- **History notes**: evacuated in 2364 during an alien infiltration
  attempt; *Enterprise-D* was scheduled here for maintenance in 2366.
- **Current commander**: **Admiral Alice Liu**.

> Full station spec mirrored in [`ships/stations.md`](ships/stations.md).
> CO bio in [`characters/supporting-cast.md`](characters/supporting-cast.md).

Three Starfleet drydocks orbit Tau Ceti Prime in the same vicinity (the
Soho Station cluster), and these are where the *Dauntless* has been
serviced.

## The Sovereign Project (classified — Captain's Eyes Only)

The classified file the manual flags as "**Captain's Eyes Only**" —
this is back-story for one of the campaign's central revelations.

### Original concept (2360s)

The **Sovereign Project** was one of three main Federation forays into
new defensive tech intended for use **against the Borg**. The
prototype, **U.S.S. Sovereign NX-73811**, was still in design during
the Battle of Wolf 359 and began testing at the time the lost Pegasus
project was discovered (Stardate 47457.1).

The project pioneered a number of innovations:

- **Regenerative shielding** — a new shield grid using phase-
  synchronised graviton polarity generators.
- **Enhanced deflector** — gravimetric distortion package for greater
  resolution on subspace scans.
- **Ablative hull armour** — a then-experimental material covering key
  hull sections (only partial coverage during initial testing).
- **Bio-neural gel packs** — first installed on the *Sovereign*
  prototype; their processing power makes the rest of the upgrades
  feasible.

### Why the prototype was shelved

- The regenerative shield system suffered phase-synchronisation
  instabilities in the polarity generators.
- The enhanced deflector drew so much power from the warp core that
  Starfleet judged it inefficient.
- The *Sovereign* NX-73811 was therefore **never commissioned** and
  was sent back to the Mars shipyards for storage and design reference.

### The successful descendants

- A second *Sovereign*-class hull was built with conventional shield
  and deflector systems.
- That second hull was christened **U.S.S. Enterprise NCC-1701-E**
  after the *Galaxy*-class *Enterprise-D* was destroyed at Veridian III.
- Many of the *Sovereign*'s pioneered technologies later found their
  way into anti-Borg ships of the Dominion War era, especially the
  **U.S.S. Prometheus NX-59650** (Stardate 50749.5), which
  successfully tested redesigned regenerative shielding during its
  attempted Romulan hijacking. Bio-neural gel packs, ablative hull
  armour, and regenerative shielding are now in service.

### The current refit (2378)

With the working tech proven on *Prometheus* and *Enterprise-E*, a
**full refit of NX-73811 is currently underway** — bringing the
*Sovereign* up to the originally intended specification:

- Larger, more powerful warp core (to feed the enhanced deflector and
  regenerative shielding without robbing the warp drive or phaser
  arrays).
- Enhanced **plasma venting** system for emergency disposal of high-
  energy plasma in inaccessible compartments.
- All main shield grids replaced with the *Prometheus*-tested
  regenerative design.
- Comprehensive ablative hull armour coverage.
- New bio-neural gel pack arrays.
- Two new EPS junctions (Deck 10 for the enhanced deflector, Deck 15
  for the regenerative shielding matrix).
- **Phased plasma torpedoes** — a new weapon project (see below).

The chief engineer of the refit is **Lt. Cmdr. Brex** (the *Dauntless*'s
own — see [`characters/brex.md`](characters/brex.md) — having received
detailed correspondence on operating the class from Geordi LaForge of
the *Enterprise-E*).

> The *Sovereign* refit makes a campaign appearance — this is the most
> important "Eyes Only" piece for the storyline.

### Phased Plasma Torpedo (research weapon)

Conceived after the recovery of the Pegasus device. Idea: shrink phase
technology into a torpedo so it can pass through a Borg cube's hull,
materialise inside, and detonate.

Engineering challenges:

- **Phasing coil miniaturisation** to fit Mark IV torpedo casings.
- **Antimatter destabilisation** of the phase coil — required a new
  warhead supplement: a **high-energy plasma infuser** drawing from the
  ship's warp nacelles, with a nanite-controlled trigger for reactant
  release.
- **Power**: the phasing process consumes large amounts of power; a
  larger-than-normal **Sarium-Krellide cell** is fitted, but the
  torpedo can only achieve a *semi-intangible* state — it can pass
  through highly concentrated energy fields (e.g. shields) but **cannot
  fully phase through hull**. This was a wartime compromise to ship a
  weapon against the Dominion; nothing functional shipped before the
  war ended.

The project is currently back in testing.

## Treaty constraints relevant to gameplay

- **Treaty of Algeron** still prohibits Federation development of
  **cloaking** technology. (This is why phase technology was pursued
  instead.) Cloak appears only on faction ships in MP/QB.
- **Cardassian surrender terms** expressly forbid the production of
  warships or fleet build-up. Galor- and Keldon-class ships still
  patrol the border and are sighted within the Maelstrom — which is a
  potential treaty-violation flashpoint the campaign hangs on.
- **Klingon/Romulan no-buildup policy** on former Dominion-allied
  powers (esp. Cardassian Union) is the geopolitical lever for major
  diplomatic incidents.
