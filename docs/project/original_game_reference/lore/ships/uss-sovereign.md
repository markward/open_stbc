## Pre-history

- Designed as one of three Federation forays into anti-Borg defensive
  technology.
- Still in the design phase during the Battle of Wolf 359.
- Began testing at the time the lost Pegasus project was discovered
  (Stardate 47457.1).
- The original prototype trials uncovered serious flaws:
  - **Regenerative shielding** — phase-synchronisation instabilities
    in the graviton polarity generators powering the upgraded shield
    grid.
  - **Enhanced deflector** — vastly improved with a new gravimetric
    distortion package, but it drew far too much power from the warp
    core.
- For these reasons, the *Sovereign* prototype was **never
  commissioned** and was returned to the Mars shipyards for storage and
  design reference.
- A second hull was built using more conventional shield/deflector
  systems — that hull was eventually christened **USS *Enterprise*
  NCC-1701-E** after the *Galaxy*-class *Enterprise-D* was destroyed
  at Veridian III.
- The *Sovereign*'s pioneered tech that *did* work was inherited by
  later anti-Borg / Dominion-War designs:
  - **Bio-neural gel packs** (first installed on the *Sovereign*).
  - **Ablative hull armour** (initially partial-coverage on the
    *Sovereign*; later proven on the *Defiant*).
  - **Regenerative shielding** (re-designed and proven on the **USS
    *Prometheus* NX-59650**, Stardate 50749.5, during its attempted
    Romulan hijacking).

## The current refit (campaign-period)

With the descendants' tech proven, the original *Sovereign* hull is
being brought up to spec:

| Subsystem | Refit notes |
|---|---|
| Warp core | New, larger, higher-yield intermix chamber to feed the enhanced deflector and regenerative shielding without robbing the warp drive or phaser arrays. |
| Plasma venting | New automatic disposal system for high-energy plasma in inaccessible compartments (e.g. plasma-fire emergency dumps). |
| Shield grid | Replaced with the Prometheus-tested regenerative shielding system. The original prototype shield grid burnt out during initial trials. |
| Hull armour | Comprehensive ablative armour coverage (the prototype only had partial coverage). |
| Bio-neural gel packs | New arrays. |
| EPS junctions | LaForge's recommendation: add one on **Deck 10** for the enhanced-deflector power routing, and another on **Deck 15** to evenly distribute power to the regenerative shielding matrix. |

Geordi LaForge's letter to Brex (manual pp. 57–58) provides the
authoritative engineering brief. Highlights:

- "Standard protocols for warp intermix ratios must be thrown out the
  airlock. The *Sovereign* class has too many command modules and
  essential systems to operate at anything less than 105%."
- A shakedown will be "grueling, frustrating and tedious, but it's
  always the best time an engineer can have."
- LaForge's parting note: "my warp core intermix phase differential is
  only **.0012 angstroms** out of alignment" — a friendly competitive
  benchmark for Brex.

## Story significance

- The *Sovereign* refit makes a campaign appearance — it is the
  highest-end Federation hull operating in the Maelstrom.
- The classified status ("Captain's Eyes Only") makes this player-
  knowledge that the bridge crew aren't supposed to discuss casually.
- Brex's pending transfer from *Dauntless* to *Sovereign* gives the
  player a tangible engineering-side stake in the refit's success.
- The Vesuvi-system location of the refit — inside the most volatile
  region of explored space, *and* the location of the Vesuvi incident
  — is no coincidence as far as the campaign storyline is concerned.
