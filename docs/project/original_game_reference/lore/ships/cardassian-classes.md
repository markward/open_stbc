# Cardassian Ship Classes

The Cardassian Union is represented by two related hulls. **Both are
treaty-restricted from production** following the Dominion War.

| Class | Length | Hull rating | Role |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Galor](#galor-class-attack-cruiser) | 372 m | 4 | Mid-sized attack cruiser (mainstay) |
| [Keldon](#keldon-class-warship) | 372 m | 5 | Heavy warship variant of the Galor |

> Treaty status (manual): *"Terms and Conditions of the Cardassian
> Union's surrender, following the Dominion War, expressly forbid the
> production of warships or the build up of military fleets."* This
> applies to both classes.

---

## Galor-class Attack Cruiser

**Mid-sized cruiser**; backbone of the Cardassian Union's military
fleet.

### Background

- Entered service in the **2340s**, originally to face Federation
  vessels of the *Excelsior*, *Miranda*, *New Orleans* and *Norway*
  classes.
- With Starfleet's introduction of *Akira*, *Ambassador* and *Nebula*
  designs, the *Galor* was quickly outclassed.
- Cardassians adapted: **increased production** and **"hunter packs"
  of three** as a doctrinal response.
- **Four known Galor variants exist**, including the **Type-3** in
  wide use throughout the 2360s.
- The larger **Keldon** class was originally adapted from the *Galor*
  design.
- Galor warships regularly patrol the Federation/Cardassian border
  and the Maelstrom.

### Maelstrom-active

> *"No data on specific Galor-class vessels, or their captains,
> operating in the Maelstrom."* — manual.

This phrasing is itself a plot signal — Galor hulls are operating in
the region but not openly.

## Keldon-class Warship

**Heavy warship.** Larger and more powerful variant of the *Galor*
class — extra hull components on top of the main body and around the
tail allow a greater crew complement and improved armament.

### Background

- The **Obsidian Order** illicitly amassed a secret fleet of
  specialized Keldon-class hulls in the **Orias system**.
- Keldon- and Galor-class warships fought alongside Dominion fleets
  during the war, inflicting heavy losses on combined Federation
  forces.
- Keldon hulls regularly patrol the Federation/Cardassian border and
  have been **sighted within the Maelstrom**.
- **Production of *Keldon*- or other warship-class hulls was expressly
  forbidden after the Dominion War.**

### Maelstrom-active

> *"No data on specific Keldon-class vessels, or their captains,
> operating in the Maelstrom."* — manual.

## Tactical implications

- **Both hulls are forward-favoured**: Galor 9/1 fore/aft asymmetry,
  Keldon 11/2. They are most dangerous head-on, weakest from behind.
- **Galor's hull rating is only 4** — relatively soft for a warship.
  "Hunter packs of three" are doctrine because individual hulls are
  fragile.
- **Keldon adds a rear torpedo** the *Galor* lacks — flanking is still
  preferable but the Keldon is harder to safely chase.
- **No cloak** on either class.

---

## Story significance

The presence of operating *Galor* and *Keldon* hulls in the Maelstrom
is **a treaty violation**. The manual's repeated "no data on specific
vessels or their captains" framing in the official archive is the
diplomatic posture; the player ship's encounters with them are exactly
the kind of evidence Starfleet would want.

Cardassian hulls are the most natural early-campaign antagonists.
