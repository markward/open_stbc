## Personality

The opening fiction quote that lands first: in his ready room (p. 2),
Wright's First Officer reports:

> *"Then I'll have our very anxious Chief Brex beam up to start a
> rundown on ship's systems and unlock the engineering stations."*

That word "anxious" is doing a lot of work — Brex's documented trait
is that he can't bear to be away from his ship.

Capt. Wright's review (1/25/2378):

> *"Lt. Commander Brex is the kind of Chief Engineer ship captains
> dream about. Not only is he a fine engineer, he is an inventive
> one. It is easy to give answers when you know them. Brex is expert
> at finding or creating answers to difficult problems, even when
> the situation is unprecedented or dangerous."*

> *"While I will hate to lose him, I believe the only place for his
> exceptional talents is on a larger ship of the line. I am prepared
> to recommend his transfer to another vessel as soon as a position
> opens up in the Fleet."*

Capt. Leland Bell's earlier review (7/23/2372):

> *"His devotion to duty — in fact, his devotion to the ship itself —
> is remarkable. Under combat conditions, Lieutenant Brex has been an
> outstanding officer. He was twice wounded in battle, the second
> time while pulling injured comrades out of danger and ignoring
> injury to himself."*

> *"The only weakness in Lieutenant Brex's record is a reluctance to
> take time away from the ship's operations and engineering concerns.
> However, I see that as a weakness that can be indulged for the sake
> of the ship."*

**Reading these together:** Brex is **the lifer**. Bolian, NCO-promoted-
to-officer, decorated for sustained excellence, decorated for combat
courage, decorated for getting wounded twice while saving people — and
quietly chronicled as someone who simply *will not leave engineering*.
"Sparkling wit" is the manual's stock phrase for what *Report* solicits
from him on the bridge.

## In-game role

Brex owns repair and power.

- **Damage control** — three repair teams, auto-assigned in damage-
  arrival order. The player can promote a damaged system to top of the
  queue (one click) or demote one currently being repaired (one click).
- **Destroyed Systems** — read-only list. The only way to repair a
  *Destroyed* system is to dock at Starbase 12.
- **Power Transmission Grid** — Warp Core / Main Battery / Reserve
  Battery gauges, total Power Used Bar, and per-system Power Allocation
  Sliders for Weapons / Engines / Sensor Array / Shield Generator (and
  Tractor / Cloak status indicators if the ship has them).

For panel layout and power-management heuristics see
[`../../ui/bridge-mode.md`](../../ui/bridge-mode.md#engineering-brex-menu--f5).

## The Sovereign refit (campaign-relevant)

Brex is **the named Chief Engineer of the *Sovereign* refit** in the
manual's *Sovereign Project* file (pp. 53–58). Geordi LaForge
(*Enterprise-E*) sends him a personal letter of advice (pp. 57–58):

> *"Chief Brex, let me begin by saying 'congratulations on winning the
> prize.' Not only is getting a ship of the line rare, rarer still is
> being assigned to the first of its class which bears the name."*

Geordi's letter contains specific operational recommendations:

- Standard warp intermix protocols must be discarded for the *Sovereign*
  class — too many command modules and essential systems to operate
  below 105% intermix.
- The refit's higher-yield intermix chamber will need extra ODN
  conduits and EPS junctions: **Deck 10** for the enhanced deflector
  power routing, **Deck 15** for the regenerative shielding matrix.
  (LaForge had two EPS ruptures on the *Enterprise-E* before adding
  these.)
- Closing salutation: *"In the spirit of a little friendly competition,
  I just wanted you to know that my warp core intermix phase
  differential is only .0012 angstroms out of alignment."*

Brex has published in the **Engineers' Subspace Network**. LaForge
remarks he saw Brex's *Dauntless*-era innovations there and would have
tried them on a *Galaxy* class himself.

## Why he matters in the campaign

- He is the named refit chief on a brand-new flagship-class hull, in a
  classified ("Captain's Eyes Only") project tied to the *Dauntless*'s
  movements through the Maelstrom.
- His weakness ("won't leave engineering") is dramatic: it makes the
  bridge crew prone to *Contact Engineering* (Saffi's menu item) when
  they need him at his console rather than head-down on a fault.
