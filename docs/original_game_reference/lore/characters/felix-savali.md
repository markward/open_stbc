## Personality

Capt. Messina's transfer evaluation (11/8/2377):

> *"While Lt. Savali has by nature a quiet personality, it would be a
> mistake to overlook him as a leader. His relationships with both
> superior and subordinate officers are cordial and positive. The
> Lieutenant has a strong and commanding manner, a quick and
> creative mind and the ability to look ahead at tactical situations,
> plotting probabilities and possibilities with astonishing
> quickness. It is not surprising he is the ship's chess champion,
> which also may be attributed to these same abilities."*

> *"Lt. Savali has been an outstandingly professional officer at the
> Tactical Station. Assigned aboard this ship upon graduation from
> Starfleet Academy, he quickly moved up in rank, gaining his
> Lieutenancy in two years. His skills as a Tactical Officer are
> exceptional, and he has made a great contribution to the quality
> of our combat record."*

> *"While I am reluctant to lose him as my Tactical Officer, I
> heartily endorse his promotion to full Lieutenant and his
> subsequent transfer to the USS Dauntless. Dauntless will gain one
> of the best young fighting officers in the fleet."*

**Reading these together:** Felix is the chess-grandmaster gunner. He
graduated 3rd in his Academy class in 2371, was decorated for tactical
skills, and reached full Lieutenant in only six years aboard the
*Strongbow*. His combat reputation is built on speed of analysis and
multi-move look-ahead — the same skill that makes him the *Strongbow*'s
chess champion. Quiet by nature, but unmistakably a leader; people who
work with him want him on their bridge.

He has been wounded in combat at least once (Purple Star).

## In-game role

Felix runs **all combat orders** — he is the busiest officer at the
console, with the broadest menu of any crew member.

- *Manual Fire* (toggle) — the player takes direct cursor-driven control
  of phasers and torpedoes; Felix continues piloting if so ordered.
- *Phasers Only* (toggle) — restricts him to phasers.
- *Target At Will* (toggle) — lets him pick his own targets and
  re-engage after a kill.
- *Orders* — *Destroy* / *Disable* / *Stop* / *Evade*.
- *Manoeuvres* — *Close Distance* / *Maintain Distance* / *Separate
  Distance* / *At Will*.
- *Tactics* — *Left Phaser Attack* / *Right Phaser Attack* / *Fore
  Attack* / *Aft Attack* / *Top Shields* / *Bottom Shields* / *At Will*.
- *Targets* drill-down — selects and expands subsystems and components.
- *Weapons* — torpedo type / spread, phaser intensity, tractor toggle,
  cloak toggle (MP/QB only).

For his complete menu tree see
[`../../ui/bridge-mode.md`](../../ui/bridge-mode.md#tactical-felix-menu--f2).

## Felix on the player's career

Felix wrote a peer evaluation for **Ens. Kiska LoMar** (Helm) at
Capt. Wright's request, dated 1/22/2378:

> *"It is essential that Helm and Tactical work together, therefore
> Ensign Kiska and I have had many 'skull sessions' on that subject.
> She has an excellent grasp of combat manoeuvres and an intuitive
> understanding of tactics. In my opinion, she should consider a
> transfer to Tactical down the line, but that will be a decision for
> her to make."*

This is a window into how the *Dauntless* bridge actually works in
practice: Felix and LoMar have been running off-duty tactics seminars
together to keep helm and weapons synchronised in combat.

## Behavioural rule worth noting

Issuing **any** of Felix's orders — even a single Manoeuvre or Tactic
toggle — while the player is in Tactical Mode hands the conn back to
him. The player must press a flight key (WASD/Q/E) to take direct
control again. Practically: the player asks Felix to fight; Felix
fights. The player flies; Felix shoots only when manually directed.
