## Personality and command style

The manual's service jacket excerpts paint a consistent picture:

> *"Her record has been outstanding in all respects. I have had no
> other First Officer so efficient and organized in all areas of
> record keeping, personnel training and bridge command. She is an
> exceptionally competent officer in regards to tactics, strategy and
> starship operations."*
> — Capt. Wittbeck, USS *Lionheart*, 6/13/2378.

> *"If Commander Larsen has one weak area, I would say it is her
> concern for exact implementation of Starfleet regulations and
> bridge etiquette. … the Commander … understands the need to allow
> for a certain amount of individual expression on the bridge and
> initiative in strategic and tactical situations."*
> — Capt. Wittbeck.

> *"I feel she could be more forthcoming in her relations with
> subordinates. She has a tendency to be somewhat rigid in her
> adherence — and therefore her insistence on subordinates' adherence
> — to regulations. I do have to say, however, that her strict
> attention to those regulations and procedures has often saved lives
> and served us well in combat situations."*
> — Capt. Lorena Damon, *Parsifal*, 5/10/2375.

> *"She has never filed a sick day. In some quarters, this might be
> considered too constant a dedication to duty, but I believe Lt.
> Larsen's record speaks for itself. As an away team commander, she
> has exhibited professional leadership qualities and concern for her
> team members at all times."*
> — Capt. Serchin Davas, USS *Arapaho*, 5/17/2368.

**Reading these together:** Saffi is Starfleet textbook. She's the
officer who has read every regulation, audits every report, runs every
drill on schedule, and has been wounded under fire making sure they
were run *correctly*. Her famously rigid devotion to procedure has
been credited with saving lives — but old-style captains have noted
her difficulty allowing subordinates the same room for personal
expression they'd like. Wittbeck states she has been actively counselled
on this and has improved.

She is the **rules-and-process anchor** of the bridge, contrasted with
LoMar's youthful exuberance and Felix's fast-thinking flexibility.

## In-game role

- Holds the **alert state** controls (Green / Yellow / Red).
- Owns the **Damage Report** roll-up across the bridge crew.
- Owns mission-info plumbing: *Objectives*, *Show Mission Log*.
- Owns priority comms: *Contact Starfleet* (Liu) and *Contact
  Engineering* (Brex).
- In Quick Battle, she has the additional setup / start / end
  controls.

## Notes on the manual

The dates Wittbeck gives (a 2378 evaluation citing three years on
*Lionheart*) and Larsen's own promotion-to-Cmdr date (2375) are
internally inconsistent if taken literally — three years on *Lionheart*
ending in mid-2378 implies 2375 as the start of that posting, which
overlaps the *Parsifal* posting. This appears to be an editorial
artefact of the manual rather than canonical inconsistency in the
universe; reproduced as-printed.
